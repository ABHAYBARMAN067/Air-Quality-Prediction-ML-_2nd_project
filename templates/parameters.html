Parameters Explained

Here are the pollutants and parameters used to predict air quality:

  • PM2.5: Fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or smaller. It can cause severe health issues if levels are high.
  • PM10: Particulate matter with a diameter of 10 micrometers or smaller.
  • NO2: Nitrogen dioxide, a gas produced by vehicles and industrial activities. High levels can cause respiratory problems.
  • SO2: Sulfur dioxide, a gas emitted from burning fossil fuels. High concentrations can affect the lungs.
  • CO: Carbon monoxide, a harmful gas from combustion engines and industrial activities.
  • Temperature: Ambient temperature, which can influence the behavior of pollutants in the air.
